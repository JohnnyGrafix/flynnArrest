Two guilty pleas in the US-Russia probe

Major legal actions stemming from the FBI probe into Russia’s US election interference, headed by special counsel Robert Mueller

Michael Flynn

Donald Trump’s former national security adviser

Resigned in February after it emerged he had lied to the White House. Former FBI head James Comey, who was sacked by Trump, testified in June that Trump had asked him to drop a probe into Flynn

Pleaded guilty December 1 to lying to the FBI about talks in December 2016 with then Russian ambassador to the US Sergey Kislyak

George Papadopoulos

A former foreign policy advisor on the Trump campaign

Admitted he tried to hide Moscow-linked contacts, made efforts to “arrange a meeting between the campaign and Russian government officials”

Arrested July 27, pleaded guilty on October 5 to lying to the FBI about Kremlin-related contacts

Paul Manafort

Trump’s nomination campaign manager

Forced to resign in August 2016 after reports he was under investigation over millions of dollars allegedly paid by pro-Russia former Ukrainian president Viktor Yanukovych

Indicted October 27. Pleaded not guilty to conspiracy against the US, money laundering, several other charges

Rick Gates

Served as Manafort’s deputy during Trump’s nomination campaign

Charged along with Manafort with allegedly hiding millions of dollars gleaned from work with Yanukovych and his pro-Moscow political party

Indicted October 27. Pleaded not guilty to conspiracy
